**What v4 fixes (both pages)**
The v3 files were a near-mechanical word-swap from the wealth source. The structure, proof, and bonus stack are still right. What was missing was *pain specificity*. A love-pain reader does not pattern-match to "money quietly leaves." A purpose-pain reader does not see herself in "near-miss, partner after partner." v4 fixes that without touching layout, CSS, JS, CTAs, ClickBank URL, countdown, or the mirror-crack interactions.

No structural changes. Text-only swaps inside the same elements. Every change is testable in isolation.

## v4 LOVE. What changed and why

### 1. Hero subhead. Replace generic "stalling your love" with pain-specific behaviour
**Was:** "The same belief stalling your love is the one quietly running your money and your sense of purpose."
**Now:** "The same belief that has you pulling back at three months, going quiet at six, ending it the moment it could become real, that belief is also the one quietly capping your money and dulling your sense of purpose."
**Why:** v3 told her *that* love was blocked. v4 names *exactly how* it shows up in her week. The reader recognises herself in the first sentence. Stefan Georgi specificity principle.

### 2. Mirror One. Love card description, sharper pattern
**Was:** "Where you pull back right before connection becomes real. The pattern that keeps love feeling just slightly out of reach."
**Now:** "Where you pull back the moment connection becomes real. The exact pattern behind the texts that go unanswered, the third date that never happens, and the relationship that ends the same way it always does."
**Why:** Concrete artefacts the reader has lived this week. Bulb 4 (will it work for me) requires she sees her actual life on the page.

### 3. Diagnostic intro. Pull the other two areas into language she cares about
**Was:** "shapes your money and your sense of purpose"
**Now:** "shapes what you let yourself receive at work, and what you let yourself want from a life that is actually yours"
**Why:** "Money" is abstract for the love-pain reader. "What you let yourself receive at work" is the same belief expressed in her language.

### 4. Mirror Block diagnosis. Match the rhythm of the wealth original
**Was:** "Connection arrives. Connection quietly drifts. The same near-miss, partner after partner."
**Now:** "Love arrives. Love quietly slips. The same almost, the same not-quite, partner after partner, no matter how different they look on paper."
**Why:** The wealth diagnosis read "Money arrives. Money quietly leaves." The verb "leaves" lands harder than "drifts." v4 uses "slips" which keeps the soft-mystical feel but adds finality. "No matter how different they look on paper" closes the "but the next one will be different" loop.

### 5. VIP box. Love Mirror Deep-Dive description, no longer placeholder
**Was:** "What your Love card means in context of all three cards."
**Now:** "Why the same near-miss keeps repeating in relationship after relationship, and the exact moment your pattern fires."
**Why:** v3 left this as a generic stub. v4 turns it into a specific promise. The Love Deep-Dive is the bonus this avatar cares most about. It should be the most concrete bullet in the box.

### 6. Path B loss-aversion. Cinematic specificity
**Was:** "in a new relationship that ends the same way the last one did"
**Now:** "sitting across from someone new who looks different but feels exactly the same, watching the third date go quiet for reasons you cannot name"
**Why:** Path B is the fear CTA. It only works if she can *see* the future she is choosing by closing the tab. v4 puts her in the scene.

### 7. Final CTA loss-aversion line. Lead with what she is actually losing
**Was:** "draining your love, your money, and your sense of purpose"
**Now:** "costing you the love that has been waiting for you, the money you have been quietly leaving on the table, and the version of your life that has felt almost within reach for years"
**Why:** "Draining" is abstract. v4 names three concrete losses with one promise that has been waiting (gain) wrapped inside the fear close.

### 8. Diagnostic closer. Order the three areas by reader priority
**Was:** "in your love, your money, and your purpose"
**Now:** "in your love life first, then in your money and your sense of purpose"
**Why:** Tiny. But signals "we lead with your pain" instead of treating love as one of three equal slots.

### 9. Path A. Make the moment-of-shift concrete
**Was:** generic "every choice in love, money, and purpose"
**Now:** "every quiet pull-back at month three, every conversation you swerve, and every relationship that ends the same way"
**Why:** Path A is the gain CTA. v3 was abstract. v4 is the reader's actual Tuesday.

## v4 PURPOSE. What changed and why

### 1. Hero subhead. Name the purpose-pain behaviour
**Was:** "The same belief stalling your sense of purpose is the one quietly running your money and your relationships."
**Now:** "The same belief that has you starting things you never finish, taking roles that look right but feel hollow, and asking the same question at 45 you were asking at 25, that belief is also the one quietly capping your money and dimming your relationships."
**Why:** v3 used the soft word "stalling." v4 lists the specific behaviours this avatar lives with. Same Georgi specificity principle as love.

### 2. Mirror Two. The Life card is the purpose reader's home card
**Was:** "Where your energy leaks and your choices keep looping. The place you feel most stuck."
**Now:** "Where the restless searching lives. The career you keep almost-but-not-quite landing in. The project you start, set down, and circle back to two years later, still unfinished."
**Why:** The Life mirror is where the purpose-pain reader's primary pain actually sits. v4 sharpens it from "energy leaks" abstraction into her actual career history.

### 3. Diagnostic intro. Pull the other two areas into language she cares about
**Was:** "shapes your money and your relationships"
**Now:** "shapes the income you stop just short of, and the closeness you keep at arm's length with the people you love most"
**Why:** Specific consequences in her life, not generic categories.

### 4. Mirror Block diagnosis. Match wealth-original rhythm + add closure beat
**Was:** "Direction arrives. Direction quietly fades. The same restless search, year after year, no matter what path you try."
**Now:** "Clarity arrives. Clarity quietly fades. The same restless search, the same unfinished thing, year after year, no matter how many fresh starts you take."
**Why:** "Clarity" is the word this avatar uses about purpose. "The same unfinished thing" names the artefact (the book, the side project, the role) she has been carrying. "Fresh starts" closes the "this time it will be different" loop.

### 5. VIP box. Re-order Life Deep-Dive to #2 + rewrite description
**Was:** Love / Life / Wealth order, with Life as generic "Where your energy is leaking"
**Now:** Life / Love / Wealth order. Life Deep-Dive description rewritten to: "Why the same restless searching keeps repeating, why the work that should fit never quite does, and the exact place the loop closes on itself."
**Why:** Lead with the avatar's primary pain card. For the purpose reader, the Life Mirror is THE deliverable. It should be the second bullet (after the Mirror Block itself).

### 6. Path B loss-aversion. Cinematic specificity
**Was:** "in another career pivot looking for the same exit you never find"
**Now:** "four months into a fresh start that already feels like the last one, asking the same question you have been asking since your twenties"
**Why:** Names a specific timeline (four months) and a specific recurring question. The reader has had this exact thought.

### 7. Final CTA loss-aversion line. Lead with what she is actually losing
**Was:** "draining your sense of purpose, your love, and your money"
**Now:** "costing you the work that would feel like yours, the closeness you have kept just out of reach, and the income you stop just short of, year after year"
**Why:** Concrete losses, not abstract drains. "Income you stop just short of" is a phrase a purpose-pain reader has thought verbatim.

### 8. Diagnostic closer. Order the three areas by reader priority
**Was:** "in your love, your money, and your purpose"
**Now:** "in your sense of purpose first, then in your money and your relationships"
**Why:** Same logic as love version. Lead with her pain.

### 9. Path A. Make the moment-of-shift concrete
**Was:** "every choice in love, money, and purpose"
**Now:** "every fresh start, every almost-right role, and every quiet pull-back in your closest relationships"
**Why:** Path A is the gain CTA. v4 puts her in her own life on the upside.

## What stayed the same on both pages (and why)

- **All 9 "See My Mirror Block" CTA buttons.** Same copy, same URL, same trust row. No reason to change a working CTA mid-test.
- **22,100 combinations math.** This is the credibility anchor for the diagnostic. It works equally well for any angle. Untouched.
- **Rebecca Hartley testimonial.** This testimonial is about *the experience of being seen by the reading*, not a specific outcome. It works for all three angles. The outcome ("conversation with my mother") reads naturally for love and purpose readers without modification.
- **James H. and Carolyn M. testimonials.** Both speak in generic-enough terms ("love, at work, with money") that they reinforce the all-three-areas claim. Swapping them per angle would be over-engineering.
- **Bonuses, pricing, guarantee, countdown, FAQ.** Same offer. No reason to vary.
- **Card order in the 3-card breakdown (Love / Life / Wealth).** Locked by the product structure and the card images. The cards themselves do not move. The *descriptions* under each card were adjusted on purpose pages where the Life card description was the most relevant pain.
